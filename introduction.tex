\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Structured pruning compresses reasoning Large Language Models (LLMs) by removing entire channel groups, but the pruned model must recover through fine-tuning.
\citet{selfhealing2025} show that reinforcement learning with verifiable rewards (RLVR) substantially outperforms supervised fine-tuning (SFT) for this recovery.
This positions RLVR as the dominant paradigm and creates a testable prediction: a pruning criterion that preserves the model's capacity for RL exploration should yield higher final accuracy.
The evidence, however, comes from unstructured magnitude pruning at 96.5\% parameter retention; whether the RLVR advantage holds under structural compression, which removes entire computational units and alters the architecture, remains untested.

In our experimental setting, this prediction does not hold.
On DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B~\citep{deepseek2025r1} pruned to 20\% MLP channel sparsity, one criterion achieves 4.4 times the peak RL reward of another (CKA-only vs.\ Standard) under Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO;~\citealp{shao2024grpo}), yet for each of the four main criteria, SFT and GRPO recover the model to within 1 percentage point of each other.
The pruning criterion determines whether RL training succeeds or collapses, but this stark difference leaves almost no trace in final accuracy.
We term this pattern \emph{reward-accuracy decoupling}.

This disconnect is surprising in light of recent mechanistic work.
RL compresses incorrect reasoning paths while SFT expands correct ones~\citep{rlsqueezes2026}.
These asymmetric pressures predict that the two paradigms should complement each other, not converge to the same outcome.
Yet existing structured pruning methods for reasoning LLMs~\citep{graspprune2026,resp2025,llmpruner2023,bonsai2025} optimize for immediate post-pruning quality without examining how the criterion shapes RL recovery.
No prior work has compared multiple criteria under RL or tested whether the ``RLVR $>$ SFT'' advantage persists across pruning strategies.

To investigate systematically, we introduce Recovery-Aware Structured Pruning (\rasp{}), a scoring function that combines gradient importance with CKA-based representational diversity~\citep{kornblith2019cka}.
A single coefficient $\alpha$ controls the balance (Figure~\ref{fig:dose_response}): the dose-response curve reveals an inverted-U in recovery accuracy, peaking at $\alpha{=}2.0$ (66.11\% on GSM8K;~\citealp{cobbe2021gsm8k}).
Under GRPO, the four criteria span a \textbf{recovery capacity spectrum} that tracks preserved diversity rather than importance: criteria that maximize diversity sustain RL training, while importance-dominated criteria fail to learn.
The SFT and GRPO curves in Figure~\ref{fig:dose_response} nearly overlap at every $\alpha$, which confirms decoupling across the full spectrum.
A depth pruning baseline (ShortGPT;~\citealp{shortgpt2024}) is the sole exception ($-$1.36\,pp on GSM8K), which suggests that pruning granularity interacts with RL trainability.

Our contributions are:
\begin{enumerate}[nosep,leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Reward-accuracy decoupling.} At 20--25\% fine-grained MLP sparsity, SFT and GRPO yield equivalent accuracy regardless of pruning criterion, supported by Two One-Sided Tests (TOST) equivalence testing ($p{<}0.05$ for 7 of 8 comparisons). At 15\%, GRPO amplifies the CKA-guided advantage, which delineates the boundary (\S\ref{sec:decoupling}).
    \item \textbf{Recovery capacity spectrum.} \rasp{} reveals that four pruning criteria produce qualitatively distinct RL dynamics. Dose-response analysis traces an inverted-U in recovery, with preliminary evidence across scales and models within the Qwen2.5 family (\S\ref{sec:scoring}, \S\ref{sec:recovery_gradient}, \S\ref{sec:additional_results}, \S\ref{sec:extended_analysis}).
    \item \textbf{SFT warmup as the dominant recovery mechanism.} At 20\% sparsity, SFT warmup recovers from near-zero post-prune accuracy to 51--64\% across all four criteria, while subsequent GRPO adds at most 0.61\,pp (1.14\,pp across $\alpha$ variants). This dominance persists at 300 steps for CKA-only and Standard. RL reward trajectories instead serve as structural diagnostics that reveal pruning deficiencies invisible to accuracy (\S\ref{sec:decoupling}, \S\ref{sec:extended_analysis}).
\end{enumerate}
